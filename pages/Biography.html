Childhood

Growing up in Stockton, CA, as a Latino meant being surrounded by a rich blend of cultures and traditions. The mornings often started with the smell of homemade tortillas or tamales, as families gathered around the kitchen table. Weekends were filled with trips to flea markets and quinceañeras, where the community came together in celebration. In the summers, kids played soccer in local parks, and family barbecues were a regular affair, with carne asada sizzling on the grill. The streets echoed with both Spanish and English, and a sense of pride in cultural heritage was ever-present, passed down through stories, music, and customs that connected generations.

Teenage Years

Growing up in Manteca, CA as a Latino teenager meant balancing school with strong family ties and cultural traditions. Afternoons were often spent helping out with family responsibilities or hanging out with friends at local spots like the community park or the mall. Quinceañeras and family gatherings were central to weekends, with music and dancing bringing everyone together. Despite the small-town vibe, there was always a sense of pride in heritage, reinforced by the mix of Spanish and English spoken at home and among friends.
